ForgeFlow Help Center

Your complete guide to understanding and using the manufacturing management system

Dashboard

The Dashboard is your command center that provides a real-time overview of your entire manufacturing operations. It's the first thing you see when you log in.

What it shows:

  • KPI Cards - Key metrics like total sales revenue, inventory value, active manufacturing orders, and staff count
  • Charts - Visual representations of sales trends and order status distribution
  • Low Stock Alerts - Items that have fallen below minimum threshold levels
  • Recent Maintenance - Latest machine maintenance requests and their status
  • MRP Summary - Material shortages, demand pipeline, and production capacity

Pro Tip: Click "Export Report" to download a comprehensive CSV export of all your data.

Manufacturing Orders

Manufacturing Orders (MFG) are the core production documents that define what needs to be manufactured, in what quantity, and by when.

How it works:

  • Created from Sales Orders when products aren't available in inventory
  • Each MFG order contains materials list from the Bill of Materials
  • Contains production stages (Cutting, Assembly, Packaging, etc.)
  • Tracks labor costs and total production cost
Sales Order → Manufacturing Order → Work Operations → Completed

Status Flow: Pending → Approved → In Production → Packed → Shipped → Delivered

Work Operations (Workfloor)

The Workfloor is the real-time production execution interface where operators and supervisors manage day-to-day manufacturing activities.

What you can do:

  • Track Operations - View all active, pending, and completed production tasks
  • Monitor Progress - See actual vs estimated time for each operation
  • Assign Staff & Machines - Allocate resources to specific operations
  • Control Flow - Start, Pause, Resume, or Complete operations

Operation Statuses:

  • ● Pending - Waiting to start
  • ● In Progress - Currently being worked on
  • ● Paused - Temporarily stopped
  • ● Completed - Finished

When an operation is completed, materials are automatically consumed from inventory!

Inventory Management

Inventory tracks all your raw materials and finished goods across warehouses and bin locations.

Key Features:

  • Multi-Warehouse Support - Track stock across multiple locations (WH-A, WH-B)
  • Bin Locations - Know exactly where each item is stored
  • Stock States:
    • Available - Stock ready for use
    • Allocated - Reserved for production orders
    • Consumed - Used in completed operations
  • Low Stock Alerts - Automatic warnings when items fall below minimum levels

Integration: Inventory automatically updates when materials are allocated to manufacturing orders or consumed during operations.

Sales Orders

Sales Orders capture customer demand and drive the entire production process through the MRP system.

Workflow:

  • Quote - Initial customer request/estimate
  • Confirmed - Customer has accepted the quote
  • Pending MFG - Waiting for manufacturing
  • In Production - Being manufactured
  • Ready to Ship - Completed and awaiting delivery
  • Shipped - On the way to customer
  • Delivered - Fully fulfilled

MRP Integration: When you confirm a sales order, the system automatically checks if you have enough finished goods. If not, it suggests creating a Manufacturing Order.

Purchase Requests

Purchase Requests (PR) handle procurement of materials from suppliers when inventory is insufficient.

How it works:

  • Created automatically when low stock is detected
  • Can be created manually from inventory items
  • Tracks supplier, quantity, cost, and delivery dates
  • Status flow: Pending → Approved → Received

Approved purchase requests can be sent to suppliers to replenish inventory.

Bill of Materials (BOM)

The Bill of Materials defines the recipe for making each product - exactly what materials are needed and in what quantities.

What it contains:

  • Materials List - All raw materials required
  • Quantities - How much of each material per unit
  • Wastage % - Expected material loss during production
  • Sub-assemblies - Pre-made components

Example:

Product: Printed Packaging Box
BOM: 1x Cardboard Sheet + 0.02L Ink + 0.01kg Glue

The BOM is used by the MRP engine to calculate total material requirements when a Manufacturing Order is created.

Staff Management

Staff module manages your employees, roles, and access levels within the system.

Features:

  • Employee Records - Name, role, department, hourly rate
  • Role Assignment - Define what each person can do
  • Current Assignment - Track who is working on what
  • Access Control - Different permission levels (Operator, Manager, Admin)

Staff can be assigned to Work Operations to track labor usage and productivity.

Machine Maintenance

Track machine health, repairs, and scheduled maintenance to minimize downtime.

What it tracks:

  • Maintenance Requests - Issues reported by operators
  • Severity Levels - Critical, High, Medium, Low
  • Status - Pending, Approved, Scheduled, Resolved, Completed
  • Service History - Full audit trail of all maintenance

Impact on Operations: When a machine is down, related Work Operations can be paused until repairs are complete.

Settings

Configure your account, company, and application preferences.

Settings Sections:

  • General - Profile, timezone, date format, currency, defaults
  • Company - Organization details, logo, branding
  • Notifications - Email and push notification preferences
  • Security - Password, 2FA, session management, API keys
  • Subscription - Plan details and usage
  • Appearance - Theme, colors, layout options
  • Integrations - Connect third-party services
  • Data - Export/import data, danger zone
